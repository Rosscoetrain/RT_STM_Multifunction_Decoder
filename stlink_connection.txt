To connect your STM32G031J6 (SO8 package) to an ST-Link V2, you need to wire four main pins between the programmer and your PCB. [1] 
## 🔌 Pin-to-Pin Connection
On the SO8 package of the STM32G031, several functions are multiplexed on the same pins. Refer to the table below for the correct physical pins:

| ST-Link V2 Pin | Target Pin (STM32G031J6) | SO8 Physical Pin |
|---|---|---|
| SWDIO | PA13 | Pin 7 |
| SWCLK | PA14 | Pin 8 |
| GND | VSS | Pin 3 |
| 3.3V (VCC) | VDD | Pin 2 |

------------------------------
## ⚠️ Important Design Notes

* Do Not Use PA13/PA14: In your Arduino code, avoid using PA13 or PA14 as GPIO (like for your bridge or LED). If you redefine these pins in your program, the ST-Link may lose the ability to connect for future updates.
* Voltage Monitoring: Connect the 3.3V pin from the ST-Link to your target's VDD (Pin 2). This allows the programmer to sense that the chip is powered.
* "Connect Under Reset": If you accidentally "lock" the SWD pins in your code, you may need to connect the NRST pin (if broken out) and select "Connect Under Reset" in your programming software.
* Boot0 Conflict: On the STM32G0, PA14 (Pin 8) is often shared with BOOT0. Ensure your PCB doesn't have a strong pull-up/down on this pin that might interfere with the high-speed SWD clock signal. [2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9]
